vehplayer video-in-Drive probe

Protocol: for each row below, tap play, watch ~15s, note the gear and approximate speed at that moment out loud (for the recording / passenger to log), then tap PASS / STALLED-OR-BLACK below that row. Do this once parked and once at real highway speed in Drive - the comparison between the two is the actual result. Row C (hls.js) is the one that matters most for the product decision.

A. Progressive MP4 (plain <video>, zero JS)
Public Google sample clip, no adaptive logic at all - the simplest possible case.
PENDING
B. Native HLS (video.canPlayType, no JS library)
Apple's public bipbop test stream. Chromium (this car's browser) has no native HLS support, so SKIP here is expected and correct, not a bug.
PENDING
C. HLS via hls.js (MSE) - the one that decides mseFallback.ts
Same test stream, fed through MediaSource Extensions into the same <video> element - exactly the mechanism a future MediaMTX-repackaged HLS pipeline would use.
